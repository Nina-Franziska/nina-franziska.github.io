Glas: Interactive Concert Visuals (2024)

In May 2024, a residency at Vega Lab and an immersive concert at Ideal Bar was the culmination of the collaboration between Glas and Uberørt, creating an adaptive stage design and interactive audio reactive concert visuals.

Software and algorithms: Touchdesigner, fluid simulations, OSC.
Hardware: Kinect Azure, Laptop, router, projector, sound card.
Collaborators: Glas (Lisbet Fritze, Louise Foo) and Uberørt (Louise Lessél, Nina-Franziska Christiansen).

Shows
Ideal Bar, May 29th, 2024, Copenhagen, Denmark.
Poolen, August 31st, 2024, Copenhagen, Denmark.
Klein, September 6th, 2024, Copenhagen, Denmark.

Exhibtions
Vega Lab, May 16th - May 26th, 2024, Copenhagen, Denmark.


The concert visuals for Glas are an interpretation of the lyrical universe of Glas’ debut album. Each song’s visuals draws inspiration from nature and includes natural elements such as oil, water, ice, smoke, minerals, crystals, metals and stars. Enclosed in a textured and analogue feel, the visuals set the scene of each song.

The stage design for their forthcoming tour was a collaboration between Glas, Nikolaj Spangaa and Uberørt, creating a modular flat-packed three screen projection setup.

Through abstract forms and organic textures, the visuals reflect both the beauty and eerie tension found in nature. Oil and water flow and merge in liquid patterns, while ice fractures, smoke moves as sentient beings mid-air, while crystals morph and move, evoking a sense of impermanence and transformation. By combining video material of each of the band members with multi-layered visuals of various colours and effects, the visuals become a tactile, textured, eerie but beutiful experience. When projected onto the three screens, the band becomes fully absorbed in this saturated visual experience.
