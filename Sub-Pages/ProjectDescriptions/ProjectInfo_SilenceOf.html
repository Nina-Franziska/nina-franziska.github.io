silence of a resonating mind (2023)

A therapeutic vacuum in between galaxies and the deep sea. Silence of a Resonating Mind invites you to explore a landscape of particles and light so invitingly abstract that it urges neural rewiring, proposing a comforting space for even the most ruminative minds.

Total length: 12 minutes (introduction 4 minutes).
Software: Desktop application running with quest link.
Hardware: Quest 2, headphones, desktop computer.
Collaborators: Composer Signe Visby Nørregård, researcher David Glowacki.

Silence of a resonating mind is an interactive audio reactive hand-tracking VR experience, which invites a moment of silence for the ruminative.

Based on theory of flow, emotional affect, and neuroscience of psilocybin therapy within individuals suffering from depression, Silence of A Resonating Mind intends to foster mental flexibility and ruminative silence while the audience moves through a soothing abstract world. Inspired by the impermanence and movement of natural phenomena, comforting repetitive generative visuals invite you to reconnect with your awareness, in stillness and by moving with intuition and care.

The experience intends to regulate the nervious system in focused attention, embracing the audience by audio reactive strings and aurora-like gradients. From stillness, they transition into exploration of wave-like bioluminescent particles, which grows to fully encompass you in galactic light. Silence of a resonating mind addresses the current need for limiting the constant mental processing of the mind and continuous stream of thoughts, which ultimately leads to rumination, stress and depression. Music composed by Signe Visby Nørregaard
