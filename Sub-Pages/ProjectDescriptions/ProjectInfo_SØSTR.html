SØSTR Immersive Installation (2022)

Spatialized 360 installation merging the intimate sound of SØSTR with cutting-edge audio reactive visuals in collaboration with 3D artist Katherine Rymer Mills from O Future.

Total length: 20 minutes, looping
Software and connectivity: Touchdesigner, OSC and Ableton. 3D asset preparation in Blender.
Hardware: Two desktop computers, ethernet, three projectors, sound card.
Materials: Holographic projection scrim.

Collaborators: Sharin Foo and Louise Foo (SØSTR), Katherine Rhymer Mills (O Future), Uberørt (Louise Lessel and Nina-Franziska Christiansen). Spatialisation support by Eric Raynaud (Fraction).

The immersive installation was developed with support from Art Hub Copenhagen.
The installation was created by the artistic duo Uberørt (Louise Lessél and Nina-Franziska Christiansen) and the band SØSTR — comprised of Sharin Foo (The Raveonettes) and Louise Foo (Gianna Factory). The collaboration became one of the first artistic residencies to transform Vega Lab, the new immersive lab in the renowned music venue in Copenhagen, into a multisensory space of projections and ambisonic audio.
SØSTR utilized xp4l spatialization software by Eric Raynaud to mix their voices and various sound sources in 3D space. The position data and sound was sent to Touchdesigner, from where the visuals could move with the music on multiple levels. 3D Artist Katherine Rhymer Mills (O Future) developed album art for SØSTR, from where elements was remixed abd incorporated as the main characters of the installation. We in Uberørt sought to saturate the ethereal voices of the Foo sisters while extending the visual landscape of O Future.
